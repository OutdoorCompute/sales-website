IT and Data Center challenges

Do you want quickly become up-to-date with the current challenges in the IT and Data Center industry? This blog post gives you an overview of the the news & research articles and other resources.

Trends

What are the general trends based on the news and research articles?:

  1. Demand for compute power keeps increasing exponentially (AI, IoT, Web3.0, Industry4.0 etc).
  2. It is very difficult to make data centers more efficient without using new technologies like immersion cooling.
  3. Heat reuse is the next logical step: Energy Re-Use Effectiveness (ERE) as an indicator becomes more important than Power Usage Effectiveness (PUE).
  4. Data Centers put additional stress on the electricity grid contributing to netcongestion.

Government policy objectives

All tiers of government recognize the necessity for enhanced computing capabilities to propel economic expansion, yet emphasize the importance of achieving this expansion sustainably in alignment with the Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs) and climate neutrality objectives.

Innovative Companies

Here are some innovative dutch companies focused on optimizing the use of heat generated by servers. They compute where heat is needed to maximize the Energy Reuse Effectiveness (ERE) and Power Usage Effectiveness (PUE).

  • OutdoorCompute: offers a Cloud plugin and Cloud Units. The Cloud Plugin enables seamless cloud integration with existing cloud providers. And the Cloud Units are installed where heat is needed, processes cloud workloads and heating buildings.
  • Hoolia: Heatwave eco-frinedly solution using excess heat from data centers for heating.
  • Leafcloud: we use technical area of several existing structures in a city to house our servers. Not transporting heat means nearly all of it can be captured and used to heat up water right in the building.
