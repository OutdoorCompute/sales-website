Technical heat specifications

The default Micro Data Center configuration consists of two compute modules that contains each 2.5kW of compute power and a 1000 liter water buffer tank. Depending on the heat recipient use case we can deliver different temperatures.

When will the "hot water buffer tank" be full?

Share your needs and we will calculate the optimal setup for you. We continuously monitor the efficiency of the Micro Data Center and adjust the parameters to achieve the highest efficiency (season, workload, customer needs etc).

The uDC will always be operational and you can use the heat of the uDC at any time. When the hot water is not used for a while (see graph below) then the maximum capacity of the "hot water buffer tank" will be reached. At this point the heat will be wasted and dissipated into the air. You will not be liable, it just ensure that the compute customers can compute on the Micro Data Center even if the heat cannot be reused.

In short, it depends on several factors:

  • Heat recipient profile: can you use the heat the whole year (eg. swimming pool)?. Or do you use it mainly during the day (eg. houses and offices)?
  • Seasons and expected ambient temperature.
  • Expected outlet temperature:
    • Aquaculture (fish farming) and Food and Beverage Industry: ~30C or lower.
    • Direct underfloor heating (housing): 30C and 45C.
    • Breweries and distilleries (fermentation or mashing process): 35C - 65C.
    • Pre-heating for central heating system (+tap water): +50C
    • Textile industry: 30C to 65C
    • Paper and pulp industries: +65C
  • Required heat capacity: the Micro Data Center can generate a maximum of 120 kWh per day (5kW * 24 hours). For example if the desired outlet temperature is 70C then the storage capacity of the buffer tank will be ~70kWh (1000KG * 4180 (Joule/KG*K) * 60 Kelvin) . That means that the Micro Data Center can buffer the heat for 14 hours, if the heat is not used within that period then the excess heat will be dissipated into the air.
  • Return temperature (cold water buffer tank): Inlet temperature of the heat exchanger.
  • Heat recipient installation: Depending on your local heat installation. For example: if your installation mixes the heat exchanger outlet water then it could be effective to generate +70C water.
